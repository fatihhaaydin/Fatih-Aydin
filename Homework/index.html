Hello welcome to my personal website.

I'm Fatih Aydın who is the MathWorks Professor of Mathematics as of 2011, the first holder of this faculty chair. I came to MIT upon completing the Ph.D. from UCLA in 1959 under the direction of Peter Henrici. I received the S.B. from MIT in 1955, and the B.A. and M.A. as a Rhodes Scholar from Oxford University in 1957. I joined the MIT mathematics faculty in 1962, as a professor in 1970. My research focuses on mathematical analysis, linear algebra and PDEs. I have written textbooks on linear algebra, computational science, finite elements, wavelets, GPS, and calculus. My video lectures are on MIT's OpenCourseWare, and I am on the editorial board of numerous journals, and am the founder of Wellesley-Cambridge Press. My service to the academic community is extensive. I served as President of SIAM, 1999-00, Chair of the Joint Policy Board for Mathematics, 1999, Chair of the U.S. National Committee on Mathematics, 2002, and member of the Abel Prize Committee, 2003-05. In the Mathematics Department, I was Chair of the Pure Mathematics Committee, 1975-79. I have received distinctions for my research, service and teaching; among them: the Chauvenet Prize, 1976 ; the Award for Distinguished Service, SIAM, 2003; the Graduate School Teaching Award, MIT, 2003; the Von Neumann Prize Medal of the U.S. Assoc. for Computational Mechanics, 2005; the Lester R. Ford Prize, MAA, 2005; the Franklin and Deborah Tepper Haimo Prize, MAA, 2006; and the Henrici and Su Buchin Prizes of the International Congress of Industrial & Applied Mathematics, 2007. I am a Member of the National Academy of Sciences and a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts & Sciences. I am an Honorary Fellow of Balliol College, Oxford. In December 2019, I was elected to be a foreign member of the Russian Academy of Sciences (RAS), in the section of Applied Mathematics and Computer Science . In 2020 I received the Irwin Sizer Award for the most Significant Improvement to MIT Education by the MIT Graduate Student Council.
